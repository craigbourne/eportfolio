Unit 1: Introduction to Research Methods. The Scientific Investigation and Ethics in Computing

Module overview

In Unit 1, we were introduced to the scientific method and the two core types of reasoning: deductive reasoning (moving from theory to observation) and inductive reasoning (moving from observation to theory).

We also explored research ethics through the Menlo Report's four principles of respect, beneficence, justice and respect for law and public interest, alongside the BCS Code of Conduct and professional practice in computing.

Required Reading

  • Fowler, B. (2023) Information Assurance and Risk Management Strategies. Apress eBooks
  • Hubbard, D. (2020) The Failure of Risk Management: Why It's Broken and How to Fix It
  • Sørensen, B.T. (2018) 'Digitalisation: An Opportunity or a Risk?', Journal of European Competition Law & Practice, 9(6), pp. 349–350. DOI: 10.1093/jeclap/lpy038
  • Kovaitė, K. & Stankevičienė, J. (2019) Risks of digitalisation of business models. Proceedings of 6th International Scientific Conference Contemporary Issues in Business, Management and Economics Engineering 2019. DOI: 10.3846/cibmee.2019.039
