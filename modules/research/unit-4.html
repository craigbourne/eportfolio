Unit 4: Case Studies, Focus Groups and Observations

In Unit 4, we studied data collection methods commonly used in qualitative research. We looked at case studies for in-depth investigation of individuals or groups, focus groups for gathering insights from 6-10 people with common backgrounds and observation methods (both quantitative and qualitative) for monitoring characteristics and behaviours.

Required Reading

  • Dawson, C. (2015) Projects in Computing and Information Systems: A Student's Guide. Harlow: Pearson.Chapter 2.
  • Priya, A. (2021) 'Case Study Methodology of Qualitative Research: Key Attributes and Navigating the Conundrums in Its Application', Sociological bulletin, 70(1), pp. 94–110.
  • Kaplan, B. and Maxwell, J. A. (no date) ‘Qualitative Research Methods for Evaluating Computer Information Systems’, in: Anderson, J. G. and Aydin, C. E', Evaluating the Organizational Impact of Healthcare Information Systems. New York: Springer New York, pp. 30–55.
  • Wohlin, C. (2021) Case Study Research in Software Engineering—It is a Case, and it is a Study, but is it a Case Study? Information and Software Technology.
